Customer Segmentation: End-to-End Process

Data Collection -> Classification -> Scoring -> Recommendations -> Execution

Phase 1: Build the Foundation
1
Collect Campaign & Customer Data QUARTERLY

Pull all active campaign data, customer records, company descriptions, and financial data from Snowflake. This is the raw input that everything downstream depends on.

Braintrust campaign snapshots core__clients finance__int_gross_profitables stg_hubspot__companies
Owner: Data Team + RevOps (Gabe)
2
Classify with AI Prompts QUARTERLY

Run Braintrust LLM prompts to classify every campaign along two dimensions. Use Case prompt assigns one of ~36 use cases (Market Research, Consumer Referrals, etc.). Company Type is determined by data model combinations of company type + use case -- no standalone prompt needed.

Use Case prompt (validated, 96% accuracy) Company Type = data model logic Grain: campaign-level
Owner: RevOps (Gabe/Mallika) + Data Team (Geoff)
3
Auto-Group into Segments QUARTERLY

Classified campaigns map into defined segments (7 messageable groups today). New use cases get flagged for manual review -- could create new segments or fold into existing ones. Data model maintained by activation/data team, not RevOps.

Research Incentives Growth Incentives Partner/Creator Claims Employee Rewards + flag new
Owner: Data/Activation Team (maintains model) | RevOps (reviews new segments)
Phase 2: Score & Enrich
4
Score & Prioritize QUARTERLY

RevOps scorecard evaluates each segment on two axes: Tremendous Success (GP, margin, concentration) and Market Opportunity (growth, TAM, new customer acquisition). Outputs a prioritization matrix (2x2) with clear ranking.

GP + margin (primary weight) H2/H1 growth index New org acquisition rate Top-5 concentration Self-serve %
Owner: RevOps (Mallika/Alex)
5
Gather Team Inputs (Sub-Scores) QUARTERLY

Each team evaluates the top segments through their lens. This enriches the RevOps score with feasibility and execution signals. Not all questions need answers every cycle -- focus on the segments being recommended.

Marketing: audience size, targetability, campaign history, nomenclature Sales: deal performance, AE strategy fit, account list quality CS: account health, expansion potential, support load
Owner: RevOps coordinates | Marketing (Val/Claire/Britt) + Sales (Scott) + CS provide inputs
FEEDBACK LOOP: Inputs refine scoring & recommendations
Phase 3: Recommend & Execute
6
Package & Deliver Recommendations QUARTERLY

RevOps delivers clear, actionable segment recommendations that account for both marketing and sales perspectives. Includes: priority ranking, recommended approach per segment (ABM vs broad), and what each team needs to do. Packaged for a non-RevOps audience.

Prioritization matrix Segment-specific GTM recommendations Enablement materials per vertical
Owner: RevOps (Mallika/Alex)
7
Leadership Alignment & Decision QUARTERLY

Head of Sales + Head of Marketing review recommendations and make the final call on segment prioritization. RevOps presents; leadership decides. Feedback loops back to refine the framework for next cycle.

Head of Sales (Scott) Head of Marketing (Nick / future VP) RevOps presents
Owner: Sales & Marketing Leadership
8
Execute Across Teams ONGOING

Each team takes the aligned priorities and executes within their domain. Marketing builds campaigns and paid audiences. Sales assigns account lists, builds SDR books, creates enablement docs. CS adjusts coverage models. RevOps monitors performance.

Marketing: campaigns, content, paid targeting Sales: account lists, SDR books, AE kickoffs CS: coverage model, health monitoring RevOps: performance tracking
Owner: Each team owns their execution | RevOps monitors
